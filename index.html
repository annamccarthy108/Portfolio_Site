1992 Barcelona Olympic Games poster recreation
graphic design class

project objectives: recreate a past Olympic Games poster, without using Olympics logo

Since we were instructed to not use any Olympic Games branding I chose to express the energy of the city through colors that reflect Barcelona (flag colors, and magnetic colors) with creative type play to show the bustling strength of the games, with a headline/article from a report on the event.

poster for the visually impaired
graphic design class

project objectives: create a 'how-to' poster for a viewer with a disability

This poster was designed specifically for visually impaired viewers. I researched guidelines on how to market towards this audience, and then designed based on my findings. I included contrasting colors, a bold typeface, and imagery so viewers can clearly understand how to use an escalator. This poster was then hung near escalators around Loyola University New Orleans.

National Student Advertising Competition - Creative Director

As creative director I was responsible for producing our overall creative direction based on research of target markets. I then constructed these visions to come to life through designing, working with copywriters, and organizing team members. National Student Advertising Competition - Creative Director

'Brings You Back' was chosen due to an overwhelming amount of research showing us that consumers associated Ocean Spray with nostalgia. Nostalgia for our target audience, millennials, is the 1990s. I created brand standards for our campaign, including typefaces, colors, and elements that must be used. These brand standards were chosen from a mix of popular 1990s colors and typefaces, Pantone's colors of the year, and hand illustrated elements from the 1990s. All brand standards were chosen to be cohesive with our creative tactic, 'Brings You Back'.
